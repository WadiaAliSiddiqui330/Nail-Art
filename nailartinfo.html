Origins of Nail Art

Nail art has ancient roots, with its earliest traces found over 5,000 years ago:

  • Ancient Egypt:

    Royalty used deep red dyes to signify status. Queen Nefertiti and Cleopatra were known for their bold nail color choices.

  • Babylon:

    Babylonian warriors stained their nails before battle to show strength and class.

  • China :

    Nail colors indicated social status. Royal families used gold, silver, and elaborate materials to decorate their nails.

Evolution of Nail Art

Nail art has come a long way from ancient rituals to a modern-day fashion phenomenon. Here's how it evolved through the ages:

  • 1920s:

    The first modern nail polish was introduced, inspired by car paint. The “moon manicure” (bare half-moon at the nail base) became trendy.

  • 1950s:

    Babylonian warriors stained their nails before battle to show strength and class.

  • 1970s-80s:

    Artificial nails, acrylics, and bold colors surged in popularity. DIY nail kits entered homes.

  • 1990s:

    Hip hop culture introduced vibrant, custom nail designs with rhinestones and decals..

  • 2000s–2010s

    Social media sparked a global nail art trend. Instagram, YouTube, and Pinterest became key platforms for showcasing designs.
